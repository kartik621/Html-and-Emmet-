Tags

Tags are the building blocks of HTML and XML documents. They define
different parts of a document and are represented by angled
brackets (< and >) surrounding a keyword. Tags are typically used in
pairs, consisting of an opening tag and a closing tag, with content in
between. The opening tag denotes the beginning of an element, while
the closing tag signifies its end.

EXAMPLE:
Tags: Paragraph Tags, Heading Tags, etc.


Elements

Elements are composed of tags and the content enclosed within them.
They represent specific types of data or structures within a
document. Elements can contain other elements, forming a
hierarchical structure.

EXAMPLE:
Div tag


Attributes

Attributes provide additional information about an element and
are placed within the opening tag. They consist of a name and
a value, separated by an equals sign (=) and enclosed in
double quotes or single quotes. Attributes are used to modify
the behavior or appearance of elements.

EXAMPLE:
href Attribute
